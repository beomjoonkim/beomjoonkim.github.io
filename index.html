My goal is to enable robots to efficiently make decisions in complex environments by utilizing their past experience and prior knowledge. I develop reasoning algorithms that can make efficient use of learned knowledge, and learning algorithms that can make effective use of prior knowledge.

I direct the Intelligent mobile-manipulation (iM^2) lab at the KAIST Graduate School of AI. Previously, I obtained my PhD from MIT, working with Tomas Lozano-Perez and Leslie Pack Kaelbling at the Learning and Intelligent Systems Group. Before that, I received my MSc from McGill, working with Joelle Pineau.
