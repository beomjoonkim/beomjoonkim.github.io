Research

My goal is to enable robots to efficiently make decisions in complex environments by utilizing their past experience. In particular, my recent research focuses on developing machine learning algorithms for robot task and motion planning problems, in which problems involve reasoning about both discrete, logical structures and continuous, geometric structures of the world. I am advised by Professors Tomas Lozano-Perez and Leslie Pack Kaelbling and I belong to Learning and Intelligent Systems Group at Massachusetts Institute of Technology
